Plastic Collection Strategy

This chart shows how the weighting parameter \( \alpha \) affects the total collected value and travel distance of cleanup vessels. The optimal balance is achieved around \( \alpha = 0.8 \).

Mathematical Model

Objective: \[ \max \sum_{t \in T} \sum_{k \in K} \sum_{i \in N_t} \pi_i(t) \cdot x_{i,k}(t) \]

Subject to:

  • Each target can be collected at most once: \[ \sum_{t \in T} \sum_{k \in K} x_{i,k}(t) \leq 1 \quad \forall i \in \bigcup_t N_t \]
  • Each vessel targets at most one hotspot per timestep: \[ \sum_{i \in N_t} z_{i,k}(t) \leq 1 \quad \forall k \in K, \forall t \in T \]
  • Vessel motion with drift: \[ q_k(t + 1) = q_k(t) + v_k(t) \cdot u_k(t) \cdot \Delta t + c_k(t) \cdot \Delta t \]
