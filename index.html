Dynamic Routing for Marine Debris Collection

Optimizing vessel paths in dynamic ocean environments with drifting plastic hotspots

üìç Problem Setting

Marine debris tends to accumulate in oceanic convergence zones. These zones contain floating plastic patches‚Äîcalled hotspots‚Äîthat vary in size and drift with ocean currents.

Objective: Maximize collected plastic value within a fixed time horizon by choosing which hotspots to visit, and in what order.

üìê Mathematical Model

Objective

Maximize total collected value over a finite time horizon \( T \):

\[ \max \sum_{t \in T} \sum_{k \in K} \sum_{i \in N_t} \pi_i(t) \cdot x_{i,k}(t) \]

Decision Variables

  • \( z_{i,k}(t) \in \{0,1\} \): vessel \(k\) targets hotspot \(i\) at time \(t\)
  • \( x_{i,k}(t) = I[ \|q_k(t) - p_i(t)\| \leq \epsilon \wedge z_{i,k}(t) = 1 ] \)

Constraints

  • (C1) Each target is collected at most once:
    \[ \sum_{t \in T} \sum_{k \in K} x_{i,k}(t) \leq 1 \quad \forall i \in \bigcup_t N_t \]
  • (C2) Each vessel selects only one target per time step:
    \[ \sum_{i \in N_t} z_{i,k}(t) \leq 1 \quad \forall k, t \]
  • (C3) Vessel dynamics with drift:
    \[ q_k(t+1) = q_k(t) + v_k(t) \cdot u_k(t) \cdot \Delta t + c_k(t) \cdot \Delta t \]
  • (C4) Start from initial position:
    \[ q_k(0) = q^\text{start}_k \]
  • (C5) Each target can be pursued by at most one vessel:
    \[ \sum_{k \in K} z_{i,k}(t) \leq 1 \quad \forall i \in N_t, \forall t \]

Score Function

\[ S_i = \alpha \cdot \left(1 - \frac{d_i}{d_{\max}}\right) + (1 - \alpha) \cdot \left(\frac{v_i}{v_{\max}}\right) \]

Used for greedy target assignment. Tunable parameter \( \alpha \in [0,1] \) controls trade-off between proximity and value.

Drift Simulation

Plastic patches are advected with ocean surface currents via OpenDrift:

\[ p_i(t+1) = p_i(t) + c_i(t) \cdot \Delta t \]

Vessel motion combines propulsion and drift:

\[ q_k(t+1) = q_k(t) + v_k \cdot u_k(t) \cdot \Delta t + c_k(t) \cdot \Delta t \]
  • \( c_i(t), c_k(t) \): current vector at time \(t\) for target and vessel
  • \( v_k \): vessel cruising speed (constant)
  • \( u_k(t) \): unit vector toward selected target

üß† Greedy Routing Logic

  1. Compute score for all available hotspots
  2. Select the one with the highest score
  3. Travel to and collect it
  4. Re-evaluate and repeat

Changing \( \alpha \) leads to very different behaviors:

  • \( \alpha = 1 \): proximity only ‚Üí short paths, but low value
  • \( \alpha = 0 \): value only ‚Üí long detours, high risk
  • Balanced \( \alpha \): optimized trade-off
