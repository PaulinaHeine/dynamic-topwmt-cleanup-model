Dynamic Routing for Marine Debris Collection

This project explores a reactive approach to the Team Orienteering Problem with Moving Targets (TOP-MT), targeting drifting plastic hotspots in oceanic environments. Each hotspot has a value, and our goal is to maximize collection within a limited time window.

Mathematical Model

The objective is to collect the maximum total value within a given time horizon.

At each step, we compute a score for each available hotspot:

\[ \text{Score}_i = \alpha \cdot \left(1 - \frac{d_i}{d_{\text{max}}}\right) + (1 - \alpha) \cdot \left(\frac{v_i}{v_{\text{max}}}\right) \]

Where:

  • \( d_i \) is the distance to hotspot \( i \)
  • \( v_i \) is the value of hotspot \( i \)
  • \( \alpha \in [0,1] \) controls the trade-off between proximity and value

Each vessel:

  1. Evaluates scores for all reachable hotspots
  2. Selects the one with the highest score
  3. Moves toward it and collects if within range
  4. Then re-evaluates and repeats
